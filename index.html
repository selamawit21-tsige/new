Addis Africa (AA) is an indigenous, non-profit, board-led Ethiopian Civil Society Organization (Registration No. 7923 with the Authority for Civil Society Organizations - ACSO). Founded in 2026 by a dedicated interfaith coalition of professionals, youth, and women spanning Muslim, Orthodox, Evangelical, and Catholic traditions, AA operates at the intersection of high growth and urgent localized shocks in urban and peripheral Ethiopian communities.

Our Identity & Mission

The Meaning Behind Our Shield of Support

Addis Africa was founded on the belief that diversity is a strength, not a division. Our founding members include:

    Religious diversity: Muslims, Orthodox Christians, Evangelicals, and Catholics working side by side

    Professional diversity: Development experts, health professionals, educators, social workers, volunteers, and spiritual leaders

    Youth and women: Significant representation of young people and women in leadership and founding roles.

To empower vulnerable populations in Addis Ababa and surrounding areas by improving access to educational materials, strengthening public institutions (schools, health facilities, rehabilitation centers, and prisons), and promoting social reintegration, while building local capacity for sustainable, shock-responsive, and gender-inclusive development.

Family Shield

Deploying direct economic, food, and emotional support to vulnerable families.

Child Advocacy

Securing clean water, access to primary education, and secure developmental conditions.
